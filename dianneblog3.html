The Difference Between Netiquette and Etiquette


In our society, where most communication is done online, social media and other platforms have become a significant part of our daily lives. The need for proper internet etiquette, or netiquette, has become more apparent than ever, but first things first, what is netiquette? Netiquette refers to how internet users should behave in online communication. It pertains to the guidelines for courteous and respectful conversations that promote positive interactions. While some behaviors are widely accepted, others may not be so clear-cut. In situations with no clear netiquette guidelines, what should be the basis for how we should behave online?

When communicating online, one of the most important things we must consider is respect for others, especially when conversing about sensitive topics like religion, politics, social issues, etc. We would always have clashing ideas, opinions, and beliefs, which leads to misunderstandings and conflict. These get worse as most of us would post whatever comes to our mind without thinking about it, then hide behind our usernames. The anonymity provided by the internet gives us the courage to freely speak our minds, which can lead to a lack of accountability for our actions. Privacy is another crucial aspect of online behavior. We find it challenging to figure out whether our information is appropriate for public consumption or should be kept private. Typically, we would see personal opinions or political views on social media platforms like Twitter and Facebook, though some of us would post those things in professional settings, which may not be appropriate. We don’t think about the platform’s purpose and audience and tend to forget about the potential consequences of our behavior. The things we post are reflections of our personality, which may have a lasting impact on our reputation and relationships.

To promote a positive community online, especially in situations without clear netiquette guidelines, we should rely on the basic principles of respect, privacy, and mindfulness of the platform that we are using and its audience. Just because we are communicating online does not mean that we should neglect the rules of respect. We should always keep in mind that we must treat others with the same level of respect that we expect to receive. We should be aware of others’ privacy and not share any information that concerns them without their consent. We should remember that different platforms have different rules, what may be acceptable on one platform may not be on another. We should always think twice before posting any content and think about how it could be perceived by others. By doing so, we can ensure that our online behavior is appropriate. Good netiquette is not just about following the rules, but also about being a responsible and respectful citizen.
