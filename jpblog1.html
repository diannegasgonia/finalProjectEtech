Why We Can't Grind Real Life Like Video Games


Why is it that I can grind a game like Genshin Impact for hours on end to get a reskin of a character I don’t even use but I can’t work on a project without feeling like I’m wasting my life away? Desensitization, in simple terms, is the process of causing someone to experience an emotion less strongly than before. In media, desensitization may occur to us when we, the audience, tend to start preferring more extreme examples of content when it is continuously fed to us through the internet. Our emotional response to something becomes less and less impactful so we gravitate towards something more stimulating. This may happen to our response to both pleasure and shock; we may become more apathetic to supposedly heartbreaking tragedies or inspiring triumphs that happen in real life.

A good example of desensitization is exposure therapy where people in fear of something are “exposed” to the thing they fear or avoid. Regularly being presented with the frightening stimulus whilst not being threatened or harmed eventually leads them to respond less and less intensely over time. But what happens when the thing we’re regularly presented with is reward and dopaminergic stimulus? Video games expose us to flashy colors, big numbers, and limited-time novelties that keep us stuck to the game loop. Games are designed with such sophistication to keep us engaged. It’s not that game developers are inherently evil, keeping us stuck in their products, it’s just that they’ve been doing this for decades and learning from us to know the most fun and relaxing features to put in their game. The stimuli and excitement we get exposed to from games are nowhere near the rewards we get in real life. Even if we know a perfect score for a test or an award for academic performance is way more valuable than any in-game item or some skin we won’t even use, we’re so desensitized to the grind of real life that it feels so miserable compared to hours in-game.

The grind in real life is boring. There are no popping sounds, cinematic graphics, or shiny objects for solving math problems. Pages don’t even turn themselves while we’re reading! We’re so desensitized by all the features we see in games that the tasks with real value seem unappealing. Games and media exhaust our brain’s reward systems that give us satisfaction for triumphing over difficult challenges because we’ve been going through those every time we engage in a tough level, a grueling boss fight, or even when we try to scroll through TikTok just to find what clip actually gets us to laugh. Desensitization occurs to us in more than just apathy towards other people’s experiences, it also hinders our motivation to work on and progress in our own lives.
