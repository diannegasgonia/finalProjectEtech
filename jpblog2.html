Carelessness Consumed Carelessly: How Creators and Audiences Fall Victim to Misinformation


Content floats around in media to be viewed by millions of people every day. Some aim to inform and do so effectively while others purposefully mislead their audience to deceive and cause harm. There is a clear separation between genuine content and harmful media when separated by the creator’s intent. Such is the case with authentic news against disinformation and malinformation (Budgar, 2023), but what about media that lacks the intent to harm yet is still inaccurate or misleading? The line between good and bad content should not be defined simply by intent alone.



Of course, honesty must be maintained in media. Information should not be fabricated, falsified, or misrepresented. Any creator with good intentions shares what they know and believe to be true. Where content tends to fall apart in terms of validity is when there is a lack of care in the process of sharing an idea. Misinformation occurs when there is no critical examination of one’s work. It is rooted in careless errors and negligence. Lack of fact-checking and inconsistency in research is present in topics ranging from cooking videos to bodybuilding tips, and can even go unnoticed in faulty medical advice. Being misinformed about a toothpick test and overcooking your cake might not be by any means harmful but wasting money on the wrong supplements, performing dangerous exercises, or poorly self-medicating can cause actual damage to a person subject to misinformation.

The audience may be at fault too. When there is not enough care taken to properly understand what is presented to them, they might take facts wrongly or spread something originally shared as satire as fact instead. Even against disinformation and faulty sources, most are not willing to spend the time checking the credibility of the content they see or who posted it.

When carelessly posted content is viewed without proper critical analysis, easily avoidable mistakes end up becoming bigger problems and real-world concerns. Content creators should observe honesty, integrity, and care when spreading information to others. Research ethics are not only for academic studies but are important in all forms of media. Furthermore, the extra effort comes a long way in producing high-quality and factual content as it comes across to viewers. It develops a community that sees the creator as credible and accountable.

As viewers, we should all be diligent and attentive about our media consumption. We don’t want to carelessly consume careless, negligently made content.
