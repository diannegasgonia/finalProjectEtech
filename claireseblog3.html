A Guide to Online Behavior


While scrolling and being on social media, sometimes we get into situations without clear netiquette guidelines. This includes what we do mostly online such as using emojis when chatting with friends or posting something, engaging in online forums or groups to talk about certain topics, and many more. Participating in online dating and free online chat websites like Omegle are also situations with no clear netiquette guidelines. How do we behave online in these kinds of situations? Where should our decisions on how we will behave online be based?

According to an article from Smart Insights, the number of social media users in the world was 4.62 billion in January 2022 and gained +137 million users in January. This shows that every year, social media users or people online increase. It is best to be aware of what’s happening online and avoid engaging in forums, online communities, and online trolls that will have negative effects on you or the people around you.

The basis for deciding how to behave online in situations where there are no clear netiquette guidelines depends on what situation you are in. For example, we use emojis and gifs when chatting or communicating with our friends, even when posting on social media. Since there is no clear netiquette guideline in this situation, we should still be aware and use proper emojis. As long as we don’t hurt someone’s feelings, it is considered proper behavior online. Another example is engaging in online forums or groups like Reddit. If the discussion or forum is becoming toxic, it is important to decide when to stop engaging. It is important to consider or think about and make proper decisions about the way that we will behave in certain situations without clear netiquette guidelines.
